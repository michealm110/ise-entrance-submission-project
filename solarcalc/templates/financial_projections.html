Annual solar savings and export revenue are based on the solar projection and actual ESB usage for the year 2024. Each year the savings and revenue are cumulatively added. The capital cost of the solar panels is depreceated over 25 years in a straight line basis. The interest rate is applied to an alternative investment strategy. The interest is compounded annually. This represents using the captial cost instead to pay off a mortgage or invest in a pension fund. For simplicity inflation is assumed to be zero.
The simulation does not currently factor in:
  • Panels should last 25 years but inverter is likely to need replaced in that time period
  • Effiency losses of panels over time
  • Interest earned on money saved due to panels
  • Decommissioning costs
  • Maintenance costs such as periodic cleaning
  • Grid connection costs
  • Behavioural changes when solar panels are installed, e.g. only heating water during daylight hours
